# Ideas

Ideas are the units of play. Each idea:
- Has a **level** (Observation through Thesis)
- Is tagged with one or more **concepts** (Rule, Faith, Meaning, Class, Art, Nature, Morality)
- Has an **orthodoxy** tendency (Apologist, Orthodox, Heterodox, Heretical)
- Has a **source**: provoked by an object, developed from a lower idea, or produced by combining two ideas
- Has **text**: hand-authored for key ideas, generated as placeholder for the rest

## Level Definitions

| Level | Name | What it feels like |
|-------|------|--------------------|
| 1 | Observation | You notice something. A surface detail, interpreted by how you choose to see it. |
| 2 | Inkling | A vague sense that something connects. Not yet a thought. |
| 3 | Idea | A clear thought, however unrefined. Can be written. |
| 4 | Concept | Structured understanding. Can be written well. |
| 5 | Argument | A defensible position with a through-line. |
| 6 | Thesis | Complete, rigorous, yours. |

## Concept Tags

Ideas can belong to multiple concepts. Tags are for our reference — the player doesn't see them. They affect which ideas can appear in combination recipes and how the ending evaluates the Written Work.

Rule | Faith | Meaning | Class | Art | Nature | Morality

## Orthodoxy

The Writer lives under an authoritarian state at war. "Orthodoxy" is alignment with the established order — the blend of state authority, social hierarchy, religious tradition, cultural purpose, and moral duty that holds the current world together.

Orthodoxy is not a separate axis the player controls directly. It emerges from:
- Which **readings** the player chooses when examining objects
- Which ideas the player combines
- Which development paths they follow
- At the Argument-to-Thesis transition, a deliberate framing choice

### The Spectrum

| Value | What it means | How it feels |
|-------|---------------|--------------|
| Apologist | Actively reinforces established power/doctrine. "This is right AND good." | Certainty. Comfort. The relief of not questioning. Rare — it takes effort to actively affirm. |
| Orthodox | Accepts the system as given. "This is how things are." | Resignation. Habit. The path of least resistance. The default mode. |
| Heterodox | Questions the system. "Something is wrong here." | Discomfort. Noticing. The crack in the wall you can't stop looking at. |
| Heretical | Rejects the system fundamentally. "This must be overturned." | Dangerous clarity. The view from outside. Potentially brilliant, potentially fatal. |

### Orthodoxy by Concept Domain

Each concept domain has its own orthodoxy axis. A player might be orthodox about Faith but heretical about Rule. Positions aren't monolithic.

| Concept | Apologist | Orthodox | Heterodox | Heretical |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|-----------|
| Rule | The state is wise and necessary. Its authority is earned. | The state is what it is. Don't question too loudly. | The state's power exceeds its legitimacy. Questions must be asked. | The state is illegitimate. Its authority rests on violence alone. |
| Faith | God ordains the order. Obedience is worship. | Faith is a comfort. The church is part of life. | Faith should comfort the afflicted, not the comfortable. | Faith has been weaponised. God is not on their side. |
| Meaning | Meaning comes from duty, service, knowing your place. | Meaning is what you make of the life you're given. | Meaning isn't given — it's fought for. The given meanings are lies. | Meaning is absent. We invent it to survive. That invention is the only honest act. |
| Class | Hierarchy is natural. The capable rise. This is justice. | Some have more, some less. It's always been this way. | The hierarchy benefits the same people. That's not coincidence. | Class is constructed violence. The economy is a weapon. |
| Art | Art should serve the nation. Uplift. Inspire obedience. | Art is fine as long as it doesn't cause trouble. | Art should tell the truth, especially when truth is dangerous. | Art is resistance or it is nothing. The pen is a weapon. |
| Nature | Nature confirms the order. The strong survive. This is law. | Nature is nature. It doesn't have opinions. | Nature is indifferent to our systems. That indifference is instructive. | Nature undoes everything we build. The only law is entropy. |
| Morality | Morality is obedience. The good citizen does what is asked. | Morality is doing your best within the rules. | Conscience outweighs law. Sometimes the rules are wrong. | All law serves power. The only morality is what you choose when no one is watching. |

## Observations (Level 1)

Each object presents a **prompt** — a raw sensory fact. The player chooses a **reading** — how the Writer interprets what they see. The reading IS the observation. It carries concept tags and an orthodoxy lean.

This means the player's philosophical position begins forming from their very first interaction. Two players examining the same gazette will produce different Writers.

## Notes

- The space EXPANDS at higher levels. More theses are possible than observations, because combination and development branch.
- Not every idea has a fixed level. Some are inherently shallow (Observations stay Observations). Most can be developed upward. Some are inherently deep (a Thesis-level conclusion that can only be reached by a specific path).
- Generated placeholder text should follow the pattern: "[Orthodoxy tendency] [level-appropriate framing] about [concept list]". Good enough to test mechanics, replaceable later.
- An idea's ID should encode its level and a sequential number, e.g. O12a (Observation 12, reading a), I7 (Inkling 7), TH3 (Thesis 3).
- Observations are the player's FIRST philosophical choice. The prompt is neutral. The reading is the interpretation.
- Most orthodox readings accept, most heterodox readings question, most heretical readings reject. Apologist readings are rare — actively affirming takes effort.
- Meaning is the most-tagged concept, intentionally. Nearly everything in this room provokes questions of meaning. That's the Writer's condition.
